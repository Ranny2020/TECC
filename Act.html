The Administrative and Traditional Organization Of Tikau Emirate

It is a Known fact that any traditional entity has its peculiar organizational and structural settings, though most emirates in the region are homogeneous in nature as a result of common origin and religious background. In the case of Tikau Emirate, Mai Tikau (HRH Alhaji Abubakar Muhammadu Ibn Grema) is regarded as the spirtual and head of the Emirate. He is also the highest commanding officer of the traditional administration of the Emirate. Right from the emergence of Tikau Dynasty, It has been a tradition that a ruler is crown through succession and endorsement by kingmakers, but when Tikau was elevated to the status of emirate, the process got some additional requirements, thus, after successful endorsement by the Tikau Emirate Kingmakers, The state Governor has final approval, subsequently the installation is done in accordance with the Tradition, culture and religion of Tikau people. Mai Tikau (HRH Alhaji Abubakar Muhammadu Ibn Grema) is the chairman of Tikau Emirate Council, which is regarded as the apex decision making body in the Tikau Traditional Settings. However, the Emir of Tikau is to Appoint District Heads (Ajiyas/Hakimai), though his appointment is subjected to final approval of the state Governor. on the other hand, Village Heads and Ward Heads are nominated by their respective district heads for final approval by the Emir. In the case of appointment of heads of Departments and traditional title holders such as: Waziri, Ciroma, Galadima, Madaki, Turaki, Talba, Wali, Kachalla, e.t.c The Emir has the sole power to appoint compentent personality, based on the norms, value and culture of the Emirate. Usually, These appointments are followed with traditional turbaning ceremony which normally holds at the Emirs Palace. Since the olden days, the Tikau traditional and Administrative setting has been highly respected and obeyed by the locals, especially considering the fact that it has been usually more accessible to the ordinary people than government officials or elected politicians. Hence, the community regarded and respect this traditional institution more than the present modern Political Institution.
